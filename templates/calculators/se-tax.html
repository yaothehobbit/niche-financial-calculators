How Self-Employment Tax Works

When you're self-employed, you pay both the employer and employee portions of Social Security and Medicare taxes — a combined 15.3% on 92.35% of your net earnings.

SE Tax Breakdown

  • Social Security: 12.4% on net earnings up to $176,100 (2026)
  • Medicare: 2.9% on all net earnings (no cap)
  • Additional Medicare: 0.9% on earnings above $200,000 (single) / $250,000 (married)
  • Tax base: 92.35% of net self-employment income (you get a 7.65% reduction)

Key Deductions for Self-Employed

  • Half of SE tax: Deductible from gross income (reduces income tax, not SE tax)
  • QBI Deduction: 20% of qualified business income (Section 199A) — most sole proprietors qualify
  • Health insurance: 100% deductible if self-employed (not calculated here)
